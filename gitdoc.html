1. Create GitHub Account

  1. Go to GitHub.
  2. Click the Sign up button.
  3. Enter your Username, Email, and Password.
  4. Click Create account.
  5. Check your email and verify your account by clicking the link sent by GitHub.
  6. Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the setup.

2. Create a Public Repository

  1. Log in to your GitHub account.
  2. Click on your profile picture in the top-right corner.
  3. Click Your repositories.
  4. Click the New button.
  5. Enter a repository name (e.g., sparrow-daniels).
  6. Select the Public option.
  7. Click Create repository.

3. Upload Files

  1. Open your newly created repository.
  2. Click the Add file button, then select Upload files.
  3. Drag and drop your files into the upload area or click Choose your files to select them from your computer.
  4. After uploading, enter a commit message (e.g., Added initial files).
  5. Click the Commit changes button to save the files.

4. Enable GitHub Pages

  1. Open your repository.
  2. Click Settings in the top-right menu.
  3. Scroll down and click Pages in the left sidebar.
  4. Under Branch, select main
  5. Click Save.
  6. GitHub will generate your site at: https://sparrow-daniels.github.io/repo/.
  7. Wait a few minutes, then open the link to see your website.
